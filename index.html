About Me

I build for the people
everyone else ignores.

"I thought I understood the problem — so I started building. I hit a dead end. That dead end became the real beginning."

I'm a final-year B.Tech Statistics student (GPA 4.30) at the Federal University of Technology Minna, simultaneously completing a Data Science degree at WorldQuant University. My work sits where statistical rigour meets real-world deployment.

I came into data science wanting to solve a single problem: how food gets moved and distributed across Nigeria — a country where 40% of perishable produce is lost to inefficiency. I built what I thought was the solution. Then I hit every wall that matters: the market doesn't work the way you assume, the traders don't trust the app, the data doesn't exist yet.

That failure became TradeFlow NG — a fully deployed agricultural trade optimization platform. Not from scratch. From experience. That distinction is everything.

Today I build production systems across agriculture, health, civic technology, and finance. The question I ask first on every project is: what does the person using this system actually need? Not what's technically impressive. What solves the problem.

Philosophy
"The best technology starts with a deep understanding of the people it serves — not the tools available to build it."
01
Start with the problem. Not the model.
Too many data scientists fall in love with their methods before they understand what's actually broken. I spend more time defining the problem than choosing the algorithm.
02
Build systems, not experiments.
A 92% accuracy model that lives in a notebook helps nobody. I build pipelines that deploy, persist, and work in real environments — not ideal ones.
03
Dead ends are data.
TradeFlow NG was rebuilt after I hit every wall. That restart — from experience, not from scratch — taught me more than any course. Failure is a valid input.
04
The best tool is the one people actually use.
I build for the person at the end of the system — the trader, the health worker, the student. If they can't use it without a manual, I haven't finished building.
