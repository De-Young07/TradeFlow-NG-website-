The Problem

Nigeria loses ₦3.5 trillion
to bad trade decisions every year

Not from poor harvests. From trading blindly — buying at the wrong market, routing to the wrong destination, returning with empty trucks.

📉

Price opacity

Traders decide where to sell based on phone calls and guesswork. By the time they arrive, prices may have dropped by 20–40%.

🚛

Empty return trips

Trucks delivering goods to Lagos return empty — burning fuel with zero revenue. An estimated 30–40% of agricultural logistics fuel is wasted this way.

🍅

Perishable losses

Tomatoes, peppers, and other perishables spoil when routes are wrong and transit is delayed. Losses of 40–60% per load are common.

📊

No data infrastructure

No reliable, real-time, inter-state commodity price intelligence exists for Nigerian smallholder traders. The information gap is systemic.

Our Mission

Built from the ground up — literally

"Every week, my uncle drives hours to sell his maize — sometimes at a loss because the price has dropped. I built TradeFlow NG so that never has to happen to him again. Or to any trader in Nigeria."

TradeFlow NG was built by a Statistics student at the university, with no external funding, as an applied research project. The system runs a Facebook Prophet forecasting model, a PuLP linear programming optimizer, and a field agent network across 8 states.

It is supervised by Dr. R. A. Adeyemi, Department of Statistics.
