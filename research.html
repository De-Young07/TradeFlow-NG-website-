Methodology

How the system works — technically

Four interlocking modules, each grounded in established statistical and operations research methods.

📋

Data Collection & Cleaning

Field agents submit weekly commodity prices via KoboToolbox — a validated mobile data collection platform used by UNHCR, WHO, and USAID globally. Raw submissions pass through a Z-score outlier detection pipeline: prices deviating more than 2.5 standard deviations from the state-commodity weekly mean are flagged for manual review before entering the cleaned dataset.

KoboToolbox · Z-score · SQLite/PostgreSQL
📈

Time Series Forecasting

Facebook Prophet is used to generate 7-day price forecasts for each state-commodity pair (32 models total). Prophet decomposes price series into trend, weekly seasonality, and Nigerian public holiday effects. The 80% confidence interval is reported alongside every forecast. Models are evaluated weekly using Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) and retrained selectively when MAPE exceeds 15%.

Facebook Prophet · MAPE · Selective retraining
⚙️

Route Optimisation

A linear programming model (PuLP with CBC solver) maximises a weighted combination of total profit (60%) and profit margin percentage (40%) across all viable commodity-corridor combinations. Constraints include supply availability, truck capacity (vehicle type dependent), perishability mixing restrictions, transport costs, and non-negativity. The solver runs weekly and produces a ranked set of trade recommendations.

PuLP · CBC Solver · Linear Programming
🔄

Feedback Loop & Retraining

Traders log actual trade outcomes (real buy price, sell price, transport cost, quantity) through the platform's feedback module. These outcomes are incorporated into the cleaned_prices table as confirmed real-world price points. A daily scheduler evaluates forecast accuracy per commodity and triggers selective Prophet retraining when accuracy falls below threshold — creating a continuously improving system.

APScheduler · Feedback loop · Selective MAPE retraining
Evidence Base

The research that grounds our approach

TradeFlow NG's theory of change is grounded in peer-reviewed evidence from agricultural market information interventions in comparable contexts.

External — Foundational

The Digital Provide: Information (Technology), Market Performance, and Welfare in the South Indian Fisheries Sector

Jensen, R. (2007)
Quarterly Journal of Economics, 122(3), 879–924
Relevance: Demonstrates that mobile phone access — giving fishermen real-time price information — reduced price dispersion by 60–70% and increased profits by 8%. Establishes the causal pathway from information access to improved trader outcomes that TradeFlow NG extends.
External — Foundational

Information from Markets Near and Far: Mobile Phones and Agricultural Markets in Niger

Aker, J.C. (2010)
American Economic Journal: Applied Economics, 2(3), 46–59
Relevance: Finds that mobile phone coverage in Niger reduced grain price dispersion by 10–16% and increased trader profits. Most comparable to TradeFlow NG's West African context and commodity focus.
External — Context

The Impact of Mobile Phone Coverage Expansion on Market Participation: Panel Data Evidence from Uganda

Muto, M. & Yamano, T. (2009)
World Development, 37(12), 1887–1896
Relevance: Shows that market information access increased farmer market participation by 10 percentage points for perishable crops. Particularly relevant to TradeFlow NG's tomato and pepper commodity coverage.
External — Benchmark

Nigeria Post-Harvest Loss Assessment

FAO Nigeria (2022)
Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations
Relevance: Provides the foundational estimate of ₦3.5 trillion annual post-harvest losses in Nigeria — establishing the scale of the problem TradeFlow NG addresses. Used in all grant applications and impact projections.
Internal — Ongoing

TradeFlow NG System Documentation & Technical Summary

Adebisi, Q. (2026) · Supervised by Dr. R. A. Adeyemi
Department of Statistics · University Project
Relevance: The formal technical documentation of the system architecture, optimization model, database schema, and forecasting methodology. Available to institutional partners on request.
Planned — 2026

Does Algorithmic Route Recommendation Change Agricultural Trader Behaviour? Evidence from Nigeria

Adebisi, Q. · Co-authors TBD

Relevance: The planned impact evaluation paper — measuring whether TradeFlow NG recommendations change routing decisions and improve trader incomes. Pre-registration and pilot data collection underway.

Impact Metrics We Track

How we measure success

These are the metrics we collect, report, and will use to evaluate the causal impact of TradeFlow NG.

MAPE
Forecast accuracy
Mean Absolute Percentage Error of Prophet price forecasts vs actual agent-reported prices. Target: below 15% per commodity.
Δ Margin
Profit margin change
Change in reported profit margin per trip for traders using TradeFlow NG recommendations vs their own pre-platform baseline.
SR%
Agent submission rate
Percentage of required weekly submissions actually completed per agent. Target: 80%+ monthly. Proxy for data quality and network health.
Route Δ
Routing behaviour change
Proportion of traders who change their planned route after receiving a TradeFlow NG recommendation. Key test of the theory of change.
BH%
Backhaul uptake rate
Percentage of traders who successfully arrange a return load following a backhaul recommendation. Proxy for logistics efficiency gain.
MRR
Monthly recurring revenue
Subscription revenue from paying trader clients. Sustainability metric — demonstrates the business model is viable without continued grant dependency.
